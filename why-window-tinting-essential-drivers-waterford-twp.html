Window Tinting Laws in Michigan: Everything You Need to Know

Driving with tinted windows can make your vehicle look sleek, provide privacy, and even shield you from harmful UV rays. But if you’re in Michigan, there’s a catch: window tinting laws in Michigan strictly regulate how much tint is allowed on different windows of your vehicle. Without understanding these laws, you risk hefty fines and possible safety violations.

Thankfully, staying compliant isn’t complicated. Michigan’s tinting laws are clear about permissible percentages and exceptions for medical needs, making it simple to balance style and legality. Whether you’re considering new tints or want to ensure your current ones meet the standards, understanding these regulations is key.

What Are Michigan’s Window Tinting Laws?

Michigan’s window tinting laws set strict guidelines for how much light must pass through your windows. These laws exist to promote safety by ensuring drivers have clear visibility and law enforcement can see into vehicles when necessary.


Key Regulations:


  • Windshield Tinting:
    • Only the top 4 inches of the windshield can be tinted.
    • The tint cannot be reflective or impede visibility.
  • Front Side Windows:
    • Must allow at least 35% of light to pass through.
  • Rear Side Windows and Rear Window:
    • There are no restrictions on the level of tint for these windows.

Michigan Window Tinting Guidelines

Window Allowed Tint Percentage Other Restrictions
Windshield Top 4 inches only Non-reflective
Front Side Windows Minimum 35% VLT Non-reflective
Rear Side Windows Any percentage Non-reflective
Rear Window Any percentage Non-reflective

Understanding these basic laws ensures you’re off to a great start in staying compliant.


Exceptions to Michigan’s Window Tinting Laws

There are specific cases where Michigan law allows exceptions to these tinting regulations.

Medical Exemptions:


Michigan permits darker tint levels for individuals with medical conditions that require extra protection from sunlight. Conditions such as lupus, photosensitivity, or other skin-related illnesses may qualify.

To apply for a medical exemption:

  • Obtain a written statement from your licensed physician.
  • Submit the documentation to the Secretary of State.
  • Keep a copy of the exemption certificate in your vehicle at all times.

Why Are Window Tinting Laws Important?

Window tinting laws aren’t just about aesthetics or personal preferences—they exist for safety and visibility.

  • Driver Safety: Excessively dark tints can limit your ability to see clearly, especially at night.
  • Law Enforcement Safety: Officers need to see inside vehicles during traffic stops for their safety.
  • Road Safety: Proper visibility ensures you can react to road conditions and other drivers effectively.

Penalties for Violating Michigan’s Window Tinting Laws

Failing to comply with window tinting laws can result in fines or legal consequences.

Consequences Include:


  • First offense: Fines starting at $100.
  • Repeat offenses: Increased fines and potential vehicle inspection requirements.
  • Illegal tint removal: You may be required to remove the tint and provide proof of compliance.

How to Measure Your Tint Percentage

Knowing your tint’s visible light transmission (VLT) is essential to staying compliant.

Steps to Measure Tint:


  • Use a professional-grade tint meter to measure VLT.
  • Test each window individually, as front and rear windows may differ.
  • Visit a professional tinting studio for accurate testing.

Choosing the Right Tint for Your Vehicle

Selecting the right tint ensures compliance while meeting your personal preferences.

Tips for Choosing a Tint:


  • Check Local Laws: Always confirm permissible percentages.
  • Consider Your Needs: UV protection, privacy, or glare reduction.
  • Professional Installation: Ensures precise application and avoids legal issues.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the darkest legal tint in Michigan?

The front side windows must allow at least 35% of light to pass through. However, there are no restrictions for rear windows and the rear windshield.

Can I tint my windshield in Michigan?

Yes, but only the top 4 inches can be tinted, and the tint must be non-reflective.

What happens if I violate Michigan’s tinting laws?

Penalties range from fines to potential legal proceedings, especially for repeat offenses.

Are there medical exemptions for window tinting?

Yes, Michigan allows medical exemptions for specific conditions. Documentation from a licensed physician is required.
